The Story of Hercules

Hercules, known as Heracles in Greek mythology, was a divine hero in ancient Greek religion and mythology. He is best known for his incredible strength and his famous Twelve Labors, which he undertook as penance for his crimes.

Key Facts about Hercules:

  • Son of Zeus and Alcmene
  • Famous for his strength and courage
  • Performed the Twelve Labors, including slaying the Nemean Lion, capturing the Golden Hind, and cleaning the Augean stables
  • Joined the Argonauts in their quest for the Golden Fleece
  • Ascended to Mount Olympus after his death

Key Events in the Life of Hercules:

Event Description
Birth Born as the son of Zeus and Alcmene
Twelve Labors Completed the Twelve Labors assigned by King Eurystheus
Death Ascended to Mount Olympus after death
